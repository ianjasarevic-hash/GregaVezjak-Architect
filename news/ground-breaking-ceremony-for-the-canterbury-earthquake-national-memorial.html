November 12, 2015 News / Ceremony

Groundbreaking Ceremony for the Canterbury Earthquake National Memorial

Work began on the Canterbury Earthquake National Memorial in Christchurch, New Zealand.

Zacelo se je delo na spomeniku canterburyjskega potresa v Christchurchu na Novi Zelandiji.

Watch Video on YouTube

Work began on the Canterbury Earthquake National Memorial in Christchurch, New Zealand. Prime Minister John Key, Canterbury Earthquake Recovery Minister Gerry Brownlee, Christchurch mayor Lianne Dalziel, and Ngai Tahu chairman Sir Mark Solomon attended the sod-turning ceremony.

Zacelo se je delo na spomeniku canterburyjskega potresa v Christchurchu na Novi Zelandiji. Slavnostnega preizkopavanja so se udeležili premier John Key, minister za okrevanje po potresu Gerry Brownlee, županica Christchurcha Lianne Dalziel in predsednik Ngai Tahu sir Mark Solomon.

The memorial, designed by Slovenian architect Grega Vezjak, will cost 11 million NZ dollars (7.22 million US dollars). It honors the 185 people killed in the 6.3-magnitude quake in February 2011, with 115 victims dying in the CTV building collapse.

Spomenik, ki ga je zasnoval slovenski arhitekt Grega Vezjak, bo stal 11 milijonov NZD (7,22 milijona USD). Casti 185 ljudi, ki so umrli v potresu magnitude 6,3 februarja 2011, od tega 115 v zrušitvi stavbe CTV.

The north bank reflective space is expected to complete by February 22, 2016, with full dedication scheduled for February 22, 2017.

Prostor za razmišljanje na severnem bregu naj bi bil dokoncan do 22. februarja 2016, polna posvetitev je predvidena za 22. februar 2017.
