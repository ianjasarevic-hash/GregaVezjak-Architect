March 5, 2017 Journal / Monument

Canterbury Earthquake National Memorial Unveiled

The Canterbury Earthquake National Memorial was dedicated in an emotional ceremony marking the sixth anniversary of the February 22, 2011 earthquake.

Nacionalni spomenik canterburyjskega potresa je bil posvecen na custvotem slovesu, ki je zaznamoval šesto obletnico potresa 22. februarja 2011.

About 3000 people gathered at the new Oi Manawa Canterbury Earthquake National Memorial on the banks of the Avon River near the Montreal Street Bridge.

Približno 3000 ljudi se je zbralo na novem nacionalnem spomeniku Oi Manawa ob reki Avon blizu mostu Montreal Street.

Christchurch Mayor Lianne Dalziel stated the memorial was "a special place where people could come to remember the devastating earthquakes that changed Canterbury." Prime Minister Bill English called it a "place of peace and a symbol of our love and respect for those who have gone."

Županja Christchurcha Lianne Dalziel je povedala, da je spomenik "poseben prostor, kamor lahko ljudje pridejo, da se spominjajo unicujocih potresov, ki so spremenili Canterbury." Premier Bill English ga je oznacil za "kraj miru in simbol naše ljubezni in spoštovanja do tistih, ki so odšli."

The memorial was unveiled by Governor-General Dame Patsy Reddy, who read a message from Queen Elizabeth II expressing hope the memorial would provide "a place to remember; to grieve for what is lost and to give thanks for what remains."

Spomenik je razkrila generalna guvernatorka Dame Patsy Reddy, ki je prebrala sporocilo kraljice Elizabete II, v katerem je izrazila upanje, da bo spomenik nudil "prostor za spominjanje; žalovanje za izgubljeno in zahvalo za tisto, kar ostaja."

During the ceremony, a marble Memorial Wall displaying the names of the 185 people killed was unveiled. First responders lifted the curtain in recognition of their rescue and recovery contributions. A minute's silence was held at 12:51pm, the exact time the earthquake struck.

Med slovesom je bil razkrit marmorni Spominski zid z imeni 185 umrlih. Reševalci so dvignili zaveso v zahvalo za njihove prispevke k reševanju in obnovi. Minuta molka je bila ob 12:51, tocnem casu, ko je potres udaril.

Oi Manawa, meaning "tremor of the heart," was designed by Slovenian architect Grega Vezjak. The memorial acknowledges those who lost lives, those injured, survivors, and the shared trauma and support during response and recovery.

Oi Manawa, kar pomeni "tresljaj srca," je oblikoval slovenski arhitekt Grega Vezjak. Spomenik se spominja tistih, ki so izgubili življenja, ranjenih, preživelih ter skupne travme in podpore med odzivanjem in obnovo.
